# Paper 3 Cover Letter Draft

Date: 2026-04-02  
Author: Codex

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, **“Do Standard Neural Networks Show RG-like
Advantages on Local Block-Spin Maps? A Benchmark-Design Negative Result on the
2D Ising Majority-Vote Task,”** for publication.

The paper addresses a recurring interpretive claim in the machine-learning-for-
physics literature: that standard neural architectures may implicitly implement
renormalization-group-like computations. Rather than advancing a broad positive
claim, we develop a controlled benchmark that asks what evidence would actually
be required to support such a statement.

Our main contribution is methodological. We show that on a Wolff-sampled,
matched-budget same-scale Ising block-spin benchmark, a standard fully
connected MLP does not outperform a linear baseline at criticality. This result
remains statistically strong after replacing local Metropolis sampling with
Wolff cluster updates, thereby removing the most obvious critical-slowing-down
objection to the benchmark. We further separate exploratory whole-lattice
transfer from primary evidence, and include appendix-level diagnostics showing
how the picture changes on a nonlinear XY coarse-graining task and in Jacobian-
spectrum summaries.

We believe this manuscript is appropriate for a venue interested in rigorous
benchmark design, careful falsification of over-broad ML claims, and the
interface between statistical physics and representation learning. The paper is
not framed as a universal negative statement about neural networks and RG.
Instead, it argues for a higher evidentiary bar and provides a concrete example
of how that bar can be operationalized.

This manuscript is original work and is not under consideration elsewhere.
Thank you for your time and consideration.
